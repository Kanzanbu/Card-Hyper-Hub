Turn-by-Turn Flow — Blackjack

Turn-by-turn Blackjack strategy breakdown
Turn / Phase Action Rule 💡 Strategy Tip
1 — Deal Dealer deals 2 cards to each player and themselves (1 face-down) Players see both their cards; dealer shows only 1 Note the dealer's upcard — it shapes every decision you'll make this round
2 — Assess Calculate your hand total Face cards = 10; Aces = 1 or 11 A soft hand (with Ace) gives you more flexibility — use it
3 — Hit / Stand Request another card (Hit) or keep your hand (Stand) If total exceeds 21, you bust and lose immediately Always hit on 11 or less. Always stand on 17 or more
4 — Double Down Double your bet and receive exactly one more card Only available on initial two-card hand Double down on 10 or 11 when the dealer shows a weak card (2–6)
5 — Dealer Turn Dealer flips their face-down card and must hit until 17 Dealer must stand on 17 or higher If dealer shows 2–6, they're likely to bust — don't risk your own bust
6 — Resolve Compare totals — highest without busting wins Blackjack (Ace + 10-value) pays 3:2 Track wins and adjust bet sizing gradually — avoid chasing losses

Quick Tips

🎯 Know Basic Strategy

Memorizing a basic strategy chart reduces the house edge to under 0.5% in Blackjack — the single best thing a beginner can do.

🃏 Card States Matter

Pay attention to what cards have already been played. In many games, tracking visible cards gives you a significant advantage.

⏱ Manage Your Turns

Don't rush decisions. Each action has consequences for the next turn — think one step ahead before committing.

📚 Study the Flow

Understanding the full sequence of a round — deal, act, resolve — helps you anticipate options and avoid costly mistakes.
